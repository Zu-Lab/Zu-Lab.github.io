Decoding cell-type specific gene regulatory programs in the mammalian brain

Thousands of brain cell types have been discovered with recent advances in single-cell technologies. The gene regulatory programs under different cell types play one of the most important roles to understand how our brain works and design drugs for neurological diseases, such as schizophrenia, depression and so on. By integrating large scale of single-cell multi-omics data from the mammalian brains, we develop computational methods to decipher the cell-type specific gene regulatory programs and identfy the core transcription factors within them.

Learn More: PNAS 2016 Immunity 2018 Science 2018

Constructing the foundation model of epigentic landscape for the mamalian brain.

L earning gene regulatory programs largely depends on the multimodal epigenetic information, such as chromatin accessibility, histone modifications, DNA methylation and so on, in the cell-type specific manner.We explore diverse deep learning frameworks on predicting epigenetic information using the raw DNA sequences and evolution information across multiple species.

Learn More: Nature Genetics 2018

D eveloping drugs usually take more than ten years and millions of dollars. We believe this will be dramatically changed due to the high-throughtput drug experiments, large-scale accummulated drug biological activties and the fast development of chemoinformatics due to artitifical intelligence. We will apply the single-cell sequencing techonolies and aritificial intelligence on drug design, including target discovery and drug modifications and combinations.
